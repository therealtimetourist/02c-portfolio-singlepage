Employment History

Kyra Solutions

May 2019 - February 2020

Contracted to resolve all ADA 508 compliance issues with public-facing E-PASS - opens in a new window and CFXWay.com - opens in a new window websites, web forms, and documents (PDFs). This was accomplished by updating exsisting code base, as well as implementing established practices for assistive technologies to work seemlessly across both websites.

Edited and documented Microsoft .Net and WordPress code bases to bring to AAA ADA compliance. As a result, both sites are now more than 90% AAA compliant.

Condensed Curriculum International

May 2019 - August 2019

Contracted to teach front-end web design/development at the Lakeland, Florida Amazon Fullfillment Center. This program set the foundation for students to pass the Microsoft MTA 98-375 Front-End Web Development Certification, providing a new career path toward web development.

Over a 4-month period, taught 150 course hours to 40 students, coaching on uses and concepts of various Internet technologies including HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery. Bootstrap and server technologies. This hands-on approach resulted in more than half the students passing the Microsoft MTA 98-375 certification test on the first attempt.

Trilogy Education Services

May 2018 - December 2018

Partnered with the University of Central Florida to coach Full Stack Web Development courses to adult students. Through this bootcamp environment, direct hands-on training was provided to students though coaching, example, and further one-on-one explanation. Graded and provided feedback on all assigned projects. Lead classroom discussions on core concepts of software engineering using modern web frameworks and technologies including: JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, jQuery, Node, React, Git, Heroku, Command Line (CLI), API manipulation and interpretation, MySQL, PHP/PHP Frameworks, Sequelize, Express, Handlebars, MongoDB.

By course end, students had become proficient in the LAMP and MERN development stacks, providing career-changing possibilities.

Vertex Solutions

February 2018 - December 2018

Contracted to AMTIS, Inc. to develop web-based training (WBT) courseware for the United States military. Worked within multiple project teams to complete SCORM web-based courses using HTML5/CSS3/JavaScript and Adobe Captivate.

All contracted courseware modules were developed and delivered on time. Contract completed normally without issue.

General Dynamics Information Technology

May 2019 - Present

Responsible for life cycle of multiple web-based training projects for the United States Veterans Administration, from initial development to deployment on secured LMSs. Websites/training courseware developed using HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, MSSQL, SCORM, and assured ADA 508 compliant.

Throughout the course of employment, coordinated multiple teams to fulfill client needs on parallel projects in a timely manner using agile/SCRUM methodology. Administered multiple internal and external Content Management Systems (CMS) and Learning Management Systems (LMS) for sharing of information between General Dynamics and Veteran Administration.

For the last two years of employment, held the role of Course Advocate that assisted with Web-based technical training of Veterans Administration employees, providing live assistance to national training managers. Responsibilities included administration, hosting, and reporting of live distance learning courses using web-based training modules created by General Dynamics, Microsoft Live Meeting, and Moodle.
